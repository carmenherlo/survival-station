FIRE: STARTING AND MAINTAINING IN EMERGENCIES

BASIC REQUIREMENTS
Fire requires three elements: fuel, oxygen, and heat (fire triangle).
Removing any one element extinguishes the fire.

STARTING A FIRE
1. Preparation: gather tinder, kindling, and fuel before attempting ignition.
   - Tinder: dry grass, birch bark, cattail fluff, fine wood shavings, char cloth
   - Kindling: small dry sticks (pencil to finger thickness)
   - Fuel: larger dry branches and logs

2. Matches and lighters: always carry waterproof matches or a lighter as primary method.
   Store matches in a sealed waterproof container.

3. Flint and steel: strike the steel against flint at a 30-degree angle to produce sparks.
   Direct sparks onto tinder bundle. Blow gently to develop an ember.

4. Friction methods (last resort):
   - Bow drill: requires dry softwood spindle, fireboard, handhold, and bow.
     Apply downward pressure while moving bow horizontally. Transfer coal to tinder bundle.
   - Hand drill: similar but uses hands to spin the spindle. Requires very dry conditions.

5. Fire piston: compress air rapidly in a cylinder to ignite tinder placed at the end.

FIRE STRUCTURE
- Teepee: good for quick ignition and cooking. Place tinder in center, lean kindling around it.
- Log cabin: long-lasting, good for warmth. Stack logs in alternating square layers.
- Star fire: push logs inward as they burn. Efficient, easy to control.
- Trench fire: dig a shallow trench for wind protection and efficient heat concentration.

WET CONDITIONS
- Look for dry tinder under logs, inside dead standing trees, or in dense canopy areas.
- Inner bark of birch and cedar remains dry longer than surface materials.
- Split larger sticks to access dry interior wood.
- Shield the ignition process from wind and rain with your body or a tarp.

FIRE SAFETY
- Clear a 1-meter radius of all flammable material before lighting.
- Never leave fire unattended.
- Extinguish completely: drown with water, stir ashes, repeat until cold to touch.
- In dry conditions, build fire on mineral soil, not leaf litter or peat.

SIGNALING WITH FIRE
- Three fires in a triangle: universal distress signal.
- Smoke signals: add green leaves or plastic for black smoke (visible in forest).
  Add dry grass or rubber for white smoke (visible in open terrain).
- Signal mirror: can be seen up to 15 km away. Aim reflected sunlight at aircraft.

FIRE FOR WATER PURIFICATION
- Boil water at a rolling boil for at least 1 minute.
- At altitudes above 2000m, boil for 3 minutes due to lower boiling point.
- Allow to cool in a covered container to prevent recontamination.
