EMERGENCY FIRST AID WITHOUT MEDICAL FACILITIES

PRIORITIES: THE ABC APPROACH
1. Airway: ensure the airway is open. Tilt head back, lift chin. Remove visible obstructions.
2. Breathing: check for breathing. If absent, begin rescue breaths.
3. Circulation: check for pulse. Control severe bleeding immediately.

SEVERE BLEEDING
- Apply direct pressure with the cleanest material available. Do not remove once applied — add more layers.
- Elevate the injured limb above heart level if possible.
- Tourniquet (limbs only, life-threatening bleeding): apply 5-7cm above the wound.
  Tighten until bleeding stops. Note the time of application. Do not remove in the field.
  A tourniquet causes tissue damage after 2 hours — only use when life is at risk.
- Wound packing: for deep wounds, pack with clean cloth and apply firm pressure for 10 minutes.

SHOCK
Signs: pale, cold, clammy skin; rapid weak pulse; confusion; rapid shallow breathing.
- Lay person flat. Elevate legs 30cm unless head, chest, or spinal injury suspected.
- Keep warm. Do not give food or water.
- Reassure and monitor. Shock can be fatal — treat underlying cause.

HYPOTHERMIA
Signs (mild): shivering, confusion, slurred speech, loss of coordination.
Signs (severe): shivering stops, rigid muscles, unconsciousness.
- Move to shelter. Remove wet clothing. Insulate from ground.
- Warm the core first: armpits, groin, neck. Use body heat, warm (not hot) water bottles.
- Give warm sweet drinks if conscious and able to swallow.
- Do not rub extremities — drives cold blood to core.
- Severe hypothermia: handle gently, horizontal position, evacuate. Heart is vulnerable.

BURNS
- Cool the burn immediately with cool (not cold) running water for 20 minutes.
- Do not use ice, butter, or toothpaste.
- Cover with a clean non-fluffy material (cling film if available).
- Do not burst blisters.
- Seek medical attention for burns larger than the palm, on face/hands/genitals, or full thickness.

FRACTURES
Signs: pain, swelling, deformity, inability to bear weight, unnatural movement.
- Immobilize in the position found. Do not attempt to straighten.
- Splint using rigid materials (sticks, boards) padded with soft material.
  Extend the splint beyond the joints above and below the fracture.
- Check circulation (pulse, warmth, sensation) below the splint every 30 minutes.
- Femur (thigh) fracture: life-threatening blood loss possible. Keep warm, treat for shock.

SPINAL INJURY
Suspect if: trauma to head/neck/back, numbness or tingling in limbs, unconscious after trauma.
- Do not move unless immediate life threat (fire, drowning).
- Keep head, neck, and spine aligned. Use multiple people to move as a rigid unit.

CHOKING
Conscious adult: 5 back blows between shoulder blades, then 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich).
  Stand behind the person, hands just above navel, pull sharply inward and upward.
Unconscious: begin CPR. Each time airway is opened, look for and remove visible obstruction.

INFECTION PREVENTION
- Clean wounds with clean water. Irrigate under pressure using a syringe or bag with pinhole.
- Remove visible debris with clean tweezers.
- Cover with clean dressing. Change daily or when wet/soiled.
- Signs of infection: increasing redness, warmth, swelling, pus, fever. Serious if red streaks appear.

IMPROVISED MEDICATIONS
- Honey: natural antimicrobial, effective wound covering when sterile dressings unavailable.
- Charcoal (activated if available): adsorbs some poisons and toxins if ingested recently.
- Willow bark tea: contains salicylates (aspirin-like). Use for pain and fever.
- Do not give aspirin to children under 16.
